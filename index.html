This is a sample microsite that includes all of my favorite little tricks. It started out as an attempt to recreate the No Business In Abuse site[1] by GetUp, hence the logo, and a few of the design elements like the maroon primary color and the centered dual navbar.

But what it became is something much more. It became a model microsite built on Jekyll and Bootstrap SASS, plus the file structure I like, the floating sidebar form, the meta tags, and even some best-practice copy/language here and there.

Sign the form on the right below to add your voice. We'll deliver every petition to Congress so your voice is heard! >>

To make this your own microsite you'll need to provide your own actual content for your campaign, apply branding in scss/site.scss or with a new Bootswatch theme, make the form work with your back-end, and update config/CNAME. You still need to build a site, but all the pieces are laid out for you.

The Jekyll/Liquid/Bootstrap/SASS setup should be easy to adapt to whatever framework you're using in production. (In that sense, refraining from using a true asset pipeline is actually sort of a feature; I'm just using sass --watch here, and cat'ing the JS with Jekyll includes.)

Next I'll probably add a floating "sign" button for the mobile styles that will zoom the viewer down to the form and drop their cursor into the name input.

You'll also get page-specific settings (via yaml frontmatter) to decide whether you want acquisition lightboxes, along with the cookie JS to determine when it's appropriate to show them.

I should probably make that "donate" link into more of a button and make it do something, like pop up a donate modal or take you to some page whose value is set in _settings.yml.

There's loads more to do. I mostly just intend for this to be a repository for all the microsite best-practices I can think of, and done in the most re-useable way I can.

Right now the site uses no custom Javascript (just jquery and bootstrap.js) and only about 70 lines of SASS on top of the bootstrap and bootswatch includes. Keeping these sorts of interventions as rare as possible should make this a useful repo for folks to copy, even if you end up replacing the Liquid/Jekyll templates entirely, or ripping the HTML and CSS as compiled.
